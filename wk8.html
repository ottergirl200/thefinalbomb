ARDUINO BOARD

The main circuit board of a device that connects and allows communication between all different parts of the system.

They possess pins that allow these connections, A0-A5 being analog and 0-13 being digital. Analog pins are either 'on' (high) or 'off' (low), while digital pins can regulate power output and adjust internal resistors.

One thing to know is that GROUND or GND (-) is necessary in all computers, whereby positive charge turns into negative. This neutralisation prevents currents from travelling in unpredictable ways.



SENSE

What a device senses is what input it recieves. Arduino reads and measures any changes of voltage, resistance, or current in the input, and manifests these in its output.



CONTROL

What the device controls is what output it creates by sending currents through the Arduino board pins. Outputs can exist in many forms, including motors, sound, and light etc.
